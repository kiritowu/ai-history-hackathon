CIVIL AFFAIRS POLICY DIRECTIVES

LABOUR POLICY

1. During the period of Military Government, there should be a single Controller of Labour for British Borneo whose duties will include the re-establishment of the Labour Department in Sarawak and North Borneo.

2. The Labour Laws which were in operation in 1941 and which were in general based on the Malayan Labour Code should be enforced subject to military exigencies. These Laws should be reviewed as soon as practicable in order to bring them into line with the Malayan Code as far as local conditions permit.

3. The flow of immigrant unskilled labour should be regulated with regard to local employment conditions as assessed by the Controller of Labour, but there should be no discrimination on racial grounds.

CONFIDENTIAL

Mr. Paskin, Colonial Office.

In the Directive on financial policy it is provided that the assets and liabilities of the municipalities will be taken over by the several Governments. This may possibly require reconsideration, but in any case a review of municipal affairs must be undertaken by this Unit, and for this purpose I attach a questionnaire detailing the information and documents which will be required initially, and which are available neither here nor in the Accounts Office.

I am not clear as to the division of responsibility and work between the Crown Agents and Messrs. Peirce & Williams, but doubtless you will be able to address particular queries to the responsible office.

This approach is, of course, only a preliminary one, and, if in addition I could be furnished with a general memorandum on the questions that have arisen and the action that has been taken in connection with municipal affairs during the last two years it would be very helpful.

May, 1944
C.A. (M.P.U.)

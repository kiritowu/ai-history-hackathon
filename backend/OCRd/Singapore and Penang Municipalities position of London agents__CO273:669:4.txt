CONFIDENTIAL

23rd November, 1943.

Dear Paskin,

With reference to paragraph 4 of Colonial Office letter No. 50744/15/42 of 27th January regarding the remuneration of Messrs. Peirce & Williams as Agents of the Municipalities of Malacca, Penang and Singapore, I enclose copies of correspondence with the firm bearing on the question of their remuneration, the rate of which has been subject to alteration at six months' notice at any time since 30th June, 1943.

We have no comments to make on the statement of their Office expenses which seems reasonable. There remains, however, the question of their personal remuneration. The total amount now being paid to the firm is 3,600 per annum and after allowing 3,000 per annum for Office expenses there is left 4,600 per annum for payment between Messrs. Allen and Marsh. We feel that we are on delicate ground here and that we are not in a position to make exhaustive enquiries into the amount of work they personally do at present. We are inclined to think that, judged purely on this basis, the amount of remuneration they are now receiving may be excessive. We realise, however, that there are other considerations which may be held to justify payments of 4,600 if this sum is regarded in the light of a "retaining fee". There is no doubt that their personal knowledge of the affairs of the Municipalities and their organization will be of great value when Malaya is re-occupied. There is also the matter of the services they may be asked to render in connection with the plans for the rehabilitation of Malaya referred to in paragraph 10 of their letter of 9th November. You will know about these but we assume that a considerable amount of work may be involved.

On the whole we feel justified in recommending that no change be made for the present in the 3,600 per annum and 3,600 per annum paid to the firm from the funds of Singapore and Penang respectively and that the question of reducing the firm's remuneration in the event of any reduction of their Office expenses should be considered when their accounts for 1943/44 have been audited as suggested in the last paragraph of Mr. Allen's letter of the

9th November, 1943.

Gentlemen,

We understand from the second Crown Agent, Mr. H. Downie, that we should submit an interim report on our work as Agents of the Straits Settlements Municipalities since January, 1942.

2. Prior to that date our work had been chiefly to call for tenders and purchase all classes of machinery and stores and to enter into consequent contracts, to advertise for, select, and appoint on agreement officers for the senior staff, and to act as intermediaries between the Municipal Treasurers in Malaya and the Chartered Bank and brokers in London regarding the Commissioners sterling investments and transactions.

3. On the fall of Malaya our duties were materially changed, partly by direct instruction and partly by inference, and became as indicated in Colonial Office letter of 27/1/43 reference 50744/15/42. The work required of our staff was to a great extent new. Because of the responsibility we were compelled by events to assume for the main funds of the Municipalities in London, in regard to which we had previously incurred no accounting duties, a new set of books had to be opened which were based on the current accounts kept by the Chartered Bank on the instructions of Singapore. In addition personal accounts were opened for all employees or dependents of employees to whom monies were disbursed. These accounts continued until the end of 1942 and have been audited by the Colonial Audit Department. As on the 1st January, 1943 all funds in this country belonging to the Municipalities were taken over by the Crown Agents and our books have now been revised to suit the accounts maintained in their office.

4. The purport of the final form of account is to show in detail all the sterling funds of the Municipalities as on 1st January, 1942, receipts and payments under appropriate heads annually since, and balances of funds in detail on closing date. It is borne in mind that the personal accounts of each stock-holder and of each member of the Provident Fund of the three Municipalities are likely to come up for investigation on the restoration of Malaya.

5. Contracts. The position of contracts at 30th June, 1943 is shown on the attached schedules for both Singapore and Penang. There still remain Indents outstanding at 30th June, 1943 to a value of 3,863 for Singapore and 1,427 for Penang.

6. Claims. With regard to the claims, the position is shown on similar schedules. From these it will be seen that no amounts have so far been recovered on cargoes diverted to Australia, the

A perusal of that rule reveals that the liability of the Commissioners is a liability to refund premiums already paid by the member and is discretionary and not absolute.

In those cases where you are satisfied on the receipt of such evidence as you would normally require, that a member, being outside enemy territory, has paid premiums on his policy, I am authorised by H.M. Treasury to inform you that you may refund the amount of such premiums to him. In those cases where the member still remains in enemy territory, it is not possible for you to make a refund, but if you are satisfied that, apart from the Trading with the Enemy Act, your powers would enable you to make payment in respect of premium direct to the insurance company, then you may do so in those cases where the member is a non-enemy subject.

I am to draw your attention to Section 15(1) of the Trading with the Enemy Act, 1939, which defines an enemy subject as (a) an individual who, not being either a British subject or a British protected person, possesses the nationality of a state at war with His Majesty or (b) a body of persons constituted or incorporated in or under the laws of any such state.

With reference to the second paragraph of your letter, where a member has taken up a policy with the Manufacturers Life Assurance Company of Canada, no special authority would be required for making a payment in this case and payment under the Trading with the Enemy Act may be made provided it comes within the authority conferred in the foregoing paragraphs, but I am to draw your attention to the fact that you should apply to the Bank of England for any authority that may be necessary under and by reason of the Defence (Finance) Regulations.

(c) To correspond with members of the Municipal staffs and their dependants and to issue salaries, allowances and Provident Fund payments in accordance with the Municipal and Provident Fund regulations, referring to the Crown Agents any question of principle or policy not covered by the regulations or by authorities already conveyed to you from this department.

(d) To maintain records of ex gratia payments to be made by the Crown Agents in respect of interest on Municipal loans.

4. For the purpose of such payments as are to be made by you, the Crown Agents will furnish you with Imprest Accounts which will be replenished from time to time. The Singapore Improvement Trust is included in these arrangements, and any payments in respect of the Trust will be made from the funds of the Singapore Municipality pending adjustment at a later date.

Draft of letter to be sent by the Colonial Office to the Crown Agents for the Colonies.

The Crown Agents for the Colonies,
4, Millbank,
S.W.1.

Gentlemen,

With reference to recent discussions in this department concerning the affairs of the Municipalities of Singapore, Penang and Malacca, I am, etc., to inform you that he has decided to ask you to undertake the custody of all the liquid and invested funds in this country belonging to those municipalities. The Secretary of State understands that you are willing to accept this responsibility.

2. The Chartered Bank of India, Australia and China has accordingly been requested to transfer to you the funds of the Municipalities of Singapore and Penang at present in their hands, and you are authorized to arrange the transfer with the bank. The Hong Kong and Shanghai Banking Corporation will be asked similarly to transfer to you the investments which they hold on behalf of the Municipality of Malacca.

3. Consideration has also been given to the position of Messrs. Peirce and Williams as agents of the three municipalities and to the respective responsibilities of that firm and the Crown Agents under the new arrangements. It has been decided that you should be authorized to arrange for payment from the funds of the Municipalities of Singapore and Penang of such ex gratia sterling advances in respect of interest on municipal loans as may be approved by the Malayan and Hong Kong Loans Committee. You are also authorized to realise securities of the municipalities as may be necessary and to exchange securities at your discretion.

4. Messrs. Peirce and Williams are being asked to continue to discharge the duties comprised in the Powers of Attorney which have been conferred upon them under the seals of the municipalities, and such additional duties as the firm has been authorized from time to time by the Secretary of State to undertake since the occupation of Malaya. In this connection I am to enclose a copy of a letter which has been sent to the firm defining their responsibilities in greater detail. On the assumption that these arrangements are acceptable to Messrs. Peirce and Williams, I am to authorize you to make funds available to meet payments made by them under the terms of the enclosed letter, and to provide for their remuneration at the rates indicated.

5. You will note that Messrs. Peirce and Williams have been instructed to refer to you any question of principle or policy not covered by the municipal regulations or by authorities already conveyed to them from this department. In dealing with such questions you will no doubt consult this Department where necessary with a view to correlating the treatment of municipal officials and their dependants with that accorded to Colonial officials of the Malayan services.

6. I am to request that you will arrange with Messrs. Peirce and Williams details of procedure in connection with the provision of impress accounts and the method of accounting. A further letter will be addressed to you regarding arrangements for audit of the accounts of these funds.

New Services devolving upon the Agents include:

1. Preparation of lists of dependents and relatives of Municipal employees and their whereabouts and payments of allowances and passages.

2. Keeping register of members of the Provident Funds and checking claims on these funds. Payments will in certain cases be requested.

3. Paying pensions to retired Penang Municipal Officers.

4. Correspondence with relatives of members of the Provident Funds regarding insurance policies defrayed out of Provident Fund monies. Also consequent correspondence with Insurance Companies.

5. Keeping registers of Stock Holders of Municipal Loans. Checking claim forms completed by applicants and reporting to the Bank and others. Answering inquiries from Banks, Companies and Stockholders regarding specific holdings.

6. In addition to inquiries from the relatives of employees to the Municipalities, requests for information from Companies, firms and others formerly resident in Singapore or Penang arise - some prefer claims against the Municipalities on the grounds of refunds owing, interest on loans and for other reasons.

7. Contracts. The cancellation of outstanding Indents has largely been finalized. There are a few important contracts on which good progress has been made towards keeping them in being and with a view to immediate action after the restoration of the Colony. Heads of agreement between parties have been settled but the final documents have not yet been agreed.

8. Lost Cargoes. Most of the claims for goods shipped and lost by enemy action have been met. Nothing so far has been recovered on diverted cargoes which have reached Australia and only a portion of the amount on cargoes delivered in India. There has been considerable correspondence in regard to these diverted cargoes. Several claims have been recovered for items dispatched by Registered Post but some are still outstanding.

Careful records of all these Indents have been kept and copies are available for the respective Municipal Departments, also drawings, reports, tests, etc.

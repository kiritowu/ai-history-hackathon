Mr. Caine.

No.11. We cannot refund deposits made in cash in respect of the Bulk Order Steel Scheme arising to absence of records (vide 60759/55 No. 5 and 7) but it seems to me that a Bank Guarantee could be released as no stock was ever delivered. Do you agree?

B. John Hall 7/4.

It seems to me more difficult for us to release this bank guarantee than it is even to refund deposits. Apart from the general uncertainty, e.g., as to whether the supplies in question may not have been shipped, or lost on the way, all the payments we are making are strictly provisional payments on behalf of the Straits Settlements Government subject to right of recovery if it subsequently appears that for any reason payment was not due. It would hardly be much satisfaction to the Banks to get a provisional release from the guarantee which could not be confirmed until communication with the Straits Government could be established.

===== Page 5 =====
In these circumstances, I would reply that, as the guarantee was given to the Straits Settlements Government, it is not possible in present circumstances to execute any formal release from it.

I must apologize for the delay in responding to your letter of 23 Nov 1943 (P/W. Jan 1957) regarding the claim of Mr. G. L. Wilson. The file unfortunately got held up with another file.

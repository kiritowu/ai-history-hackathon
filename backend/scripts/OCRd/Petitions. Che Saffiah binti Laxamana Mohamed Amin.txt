DRAFT.

THE RIGHT HON.

C.R. ATTLEE, M.P.

I return the letter from Che Saffian binti Laxamana Mohamed Amin which you sent me on the 11th March.

Petitions from this lady have reached me more than once before and after careful examination of the circumstances of her case, in the light of report from the High Commissioner for the Malay States, I caused her to be informed that I see no reason to intervene on her behalf.

Briefly the facts are that Che Saffiah binti Laxamana Mohamed Amin is the daughter of a Malay Chief who was one of the signatories of the Treaty of Pangkor when Perak came under British Protection and one of the three Perak Chiefs who accompanied the ex-Sultan Abdullah on his exile to the Seychelles following/

===== Page 7 =====
following upon the murder of the first British Resident. On the death of this Chief his widow and her three sons and daughter (the last being the petitioner) were in 1904 granted compassionate allowances on the recommendation of the State Council, on the understanding that they should cease when the sons reached the age of 21 and the daughter should marry.

Che Saffiah married a Johore Government Officer, who died 4 years ago, and on her marriage the compassionate allowance paid to her ceased in accordance with the condition laid down at the time of the grant. She twice petitioned the High Commissioner's predecessors for the continuance of her allowance, but they were not prepared to reconsider the matter.

Petitions for a political pension (after the attainment of his majority) were also received from one of her brothers, but after consideration it was decided not to intervene on his behalf.

The High Commissioner does not feel that Che Saffiah's case calls for exceptional treatment and the Government of Perak is firmly opposed to the grant of any pension however small, as being likely to give rise to a number of similar claims.
